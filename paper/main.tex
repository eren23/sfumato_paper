% ---------- Abstract ----------
\begin{abstract}
Hybrid pipelines that combine an autoregressive (AR) language model
with a discrete-diffusion language model (DDLM) for chain-of-thought
reasoning have produced mixed results in the literature, with
conflicting reports on whether AR planning helps DDLM denoising and
whether DDLM sampling diversity is useful.  We argue these conflicts
collapse once the failure surface is decomposed: hybrid AR/DDLM
reasoning fails along \emph{at least three orthogonal axes} ---
interface-format brittleness, planner-content trust, and
sampling-diversity preservation --- that can be characterized and
intervened on independently.  On GSM8K~\citep{cobbe2021gsm8k} with
\LLaDA{}-8B-Instruct~\citep{nie2025llada} and
\Qwen{\{0.5B,1.5B\}}-Instruct~\citep{qwen2024report} we show: (i)
interface-format brittleness is fixable with a small
($r{=}8$)~\citep{hu2022lora} prefix-robustness LoRA, with a
measurable capacity tradeoff between no-prefix accuracy and
branch-vote ceiling; (ii) planner-content trust is
capacity-dependent in opposite directions across planners --- a
$0.5$B planner improves at higher capacity ($+5$\,pp), while a
$1.5$B planner regresses by $13$\,pp; (iii)
format-augmented LoRA training \emph{expands} sampling diversity
($5/5$-branch agreement $51.5\% \rightarrow 47.5\%$), rather than
collapsing it; (iv) consensus distillation into the diffusion model
is design-sensitive, not architecture-limited: late-block answer-span
surgery fails to bridge the \ctwoc{}-vs-\cmaj{} gap (\ctwoc{}$=70.5\%$
across two iterations); earlier-block full-response surgery recovers
(\ctwoc{}$=79.0\%$, within sampling error of the $80\%$ pre-registered
target).  Disentangling ablations attribute most of the lift
($+4$\,pp) to where commit fires in the diffusion schedule, with
full-response training a smaller secondary effect ($+2$\,pp).
Branch aggregation (\cmajc{}) on top of the \ctwoc{}-fixed v3 design
reaches $82.5\%$, $+3.5$\,pp over base test \cmaj{}, indicating
param-time and compute-time consensus mechanisms compose rather than
substitute.
A \Qwen{0.5B} self-consistency baseline~\citep{wang2023selfconsistency}
($b{=}5$, $t{=}0.7$) reaches only $40.5\%$ on the same eval, vs
\LLaDA{}'s $79\text{--}82\%$, ruling out generic self-consistency as
the explanation for the diffusion \cmaj{} advantage.

All datasets and adapters are public on the Hugging Face Hub.
Total compute spend on a single RTX~4090: $\approx \$3.50$ across all
experiments.
\end{abstract}
